SPONGE FLOOR PLAN

Sponge, Acrylic paint
90 x 60 (cm)
Independent work, 2019 Summer

An experiment to find a two-dimensional schema from a three-dimensional object. Going from plane to threedimensional is progressive, but going from three-dimensional to flat is retrograde. I created floor plan by rolling sponge with its side and plane. This work is a base floor plan for upcoming architecture models I created.

×

MODEL 1: PEDITECTURE

Foam board, Clay mixed with oil
900 x 200 (mm)
Independent work, 2019 Summer

Unlike any other type of arts, architecture is an area that can be recognized by walking on its own feet rather than by simply seeing. In that sense, I believe that the perception of architecture and space begins with the feet. Using the sponge floor plan from previous work, I created the first model by stepping on the clay with my full weight, gravity and senses. Architecture which is made by stepping is the most essential over any other architectures made by brain and hands. In addition, the intrinsic space attracts people, so they are engraved with new footprints after all.
